Section 01

Problem Statement

Current standing and open space allocations in IARS are manually managed. Decisions rely on spreadsheets, institutional knowledge, and reactive processes — resulting in underutilization of standings, inconsistent open-space adjustments, limited forecasting, and lack of proactive alerts.

Manual Allocation Management

Standing and open space allocations managed through spreadsheets and institutional knowledge. Terminal operators make gut-feeling decisions with no systematic forecasting. When an experienced operator leaves, their knowledge goes with them.

Emotion-Based Decisions

Allocation decisions influenced by individual judgment, relationships, and reactive processes rather than data-driven analysis. No standardized methodology — decisions vary wildly across terminals and operators.

Underutilization of Standings

Premium customers hold standing allocations reserved whether used or not. With no-show rates as high as 64.75%, substantial train capacity is phantom-blocked — unavailable to other customers but never actually used.

Limited Forecasting & No Proactive Alerts

All decisions are backward-looking. No demand forecasting exists. No anomaly detection. Overbooking discovered only when containers arrive; underutilization identified only after trains depart with empty slots.

⚠
The Core Insight: Reservations drastically overstate actual demand. With no-show rates exceeding 60%, the majority of reserved capacity never materializes. The system must forecast net effective demand (actual ingates), not gross reservations.

Section 04

Technical Approach — Decomposed Modeling

Container volume comprises distinct load types with different predictability. Our decomposed approach separates these into transparent business components for superior explainability, accuracy, and operator trust.

Why Decomposition Matters

Load TypePredictabilityProbable Model ApproachesUncertainty
Standing AllocationsVery High (contractual, stable)Customer utilization rates, historical averages, classification models±3-5%
Recurring IndustryMedium (known schedules, seasonal)Time series decomposition, calendar-based, seasonality-aware methods±8-12%
Spot Market / One-OffsLow (inherently variable)Gradient boosting ensembles, trend analysis, ensemble methods±20-40%
📊
Model Flexibility: We intentionally avoid hard-coding specific ML algorithms. The framework is model-agnostic — the decomposed architecture defines what to predict, not how. Algorithms selected, evaluated, and swapped based on actual data characteristics. Probable families: time-series, gradient boosting, ensembles, deep learning — final selection is data-driven.

Explainability Through Decomposition

✅ Decomposed Approach (Proposed)

Probable Algorithm Families

Candidates — final selection determined by data characteristics in Phase 0. Models may be evaluated, ensembled, or swapped over time.

Time Series Models

Seasonal decomposition, trend detection, holiday effects. Candidates: ARIMA, Prophet, exponential smoothing, or similar.

Gradient Boosting Ensembles

Multi-feature tabular data. Feature importance via SHAP. Candidates: XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost, or similar.

Ensemble / Stacking

Meta-learner combining multiple models. Reduces variance. Production predictions leverage diverse model strengths.

Anomaly Detection

Unsupervised spike/drop detection. Candidates: Isolation Forest, CUSUM, Z-score, or similar.

Deep Learning (Phase 2+)

Complex temporal dependencies. Candidates: LSTM, Temporal Fusion Transformer.

LLM Integration (Azure OpenAI)

Post-ML layer: converts model outputs into narratives, powers chat interface. Not for prediction — for communication.

Section 06

AI/ML → LLM Pipeline

Raw data flows through feature engineering and ML models to produce structured predictions, then passes through an Azure OpenAI LLM layer that transforms raw output into meaningful, validated, human-readable recommendations.

🧠
Key Architecture Decision: ML models produce structured numerical predictions. The LLM layer (Azure OpenAI) sits after ML inference — it does NOT make predictions. Instead, it generates natural-language explanations, validates output coherence, and powers the conversational interface. Prediction accuracy from specialized ML; operator-friendly communication through LLM.
