Section 01

Problem Statement

The IARS reservation system does not give operations a true sense of what is actually going to happen. Allocations over-represent demand, standings are underutilized, and every decision today is reactive, manual, and backward-looking.

Allocations ≠ Reality

Standing allocations are set contractually (e.g., 50/day, 7 days/week) even though actual customer behavior varies dramatically by day-of-week. Thursday may use 100% while Monday uses 30%. The reservation system shows "full" when the train is actually half-empty.

Manual, Reactive Process

The INOC (centralized operations team) manually monitors allocations using spreadsheets and institutional knowledge. When they're busy or unavailable, opportunities are simply missed — space that could have been opened goes unused.

No Forecasting Exists

Nobody looks forward. Operators compare this week to last week at best. Year-over-year trends, seasonality, customer shifts, and day-of-week patterns across months are too much data for humans to analyze manually at scale.

Multi-Hop Response Time

When customers need space: customer → sales rep → INOC → evaluation → maybe add space. By the time space is added, the gate cutoff may have passed. Every hour of delay reduces utilization of added space.

⚠
The fundamental gap: Operations needs to know "How many containers should I actually expect on this train on this day?" — not what's allocated, not what's reserved, but what will physically arrive at the gate. Today, nobody has that answer until after the fact.

Section 02

The Core Prediction Question

Everything in this system reduces to one question, asked per train, per day:

"For Train I010 departing Bedford Park on Thursday March 5th — how many containers should we actually expect?"

This is not capacity planning. We are not setting train capacity — that is driven by physical constraints (length, weight, tonnage, network). We are predicting customer demand: how many containers will physically arrive at the gate for this train on this day.

Why This Matters to Operations

If We Predict Demand Accurately

✓INOC knows which trains have room for more open space
✓Standby customers get space earlier (not at the last minute)
✓Standing underutilization is quantified per customer, per day
✓The "sweet spot" for adding space (hours before cutoff) is data-driven
✓No more missed revenue because one person was away from their desk

What We Are NOT Doing

✗Not setting train length, weight, or physical capacity
✗Not replacing commercial standing agreements
✗Not auto-changing customer allocations daily
✗Not bypassing INOC — they approve every change
✗Not running 400 micro-recommendations per day

Two-Tier Prediction Frequency

TierFrequencyPurposeAudienceVolume
StrategicWeekly or on-demandBroader trends: customer behavior shifts, standing utilization patterns, seasonal rebalancing recommendationsINOC leadership, Commercial teamNetwork-wide summary

⚠
Significance threshold: Not every prediction change deserves a recommendation. If the model predicts 2 more containers than allocated — that's noise. The system only surfaces recommendations when the predicted gap exceeds a configurable threshold (e.g., ≥5 containers or ≥10% of allocation).
Section 03

Temporal Dimensions — How the Prediction Thinks

Before the model looks at any data, it must first understand what kind of day it's predicting for. Six temporal dimensions anchor every prediction — with day-of-week as the dominant signal.

Dimension 1
Day of Week
Thursday vs. Saturday is the single biggest predictor. All comparisons snap to the same weekday.
Dimension 2
Week Recency
Same day last week, 2 weeks ago, 4 weeks ago. Weighted toward most recent. Is it trending?
Dimension 3
Month Position
"3rd Thursday of March" — not "March 20th." Position within the month matters, not the calendar date.
Dimension 4
Seasonality
Q4 peak (Oct–Dec), summer patterns, quarterly cycles. "Nearest Thursday to this day last year."
Dimension 5
Holiday Override
Holidays take precedence over all. Thanksgiving, Christmas, July 4th — each with unique impact curves (pre/post/during).
Dimension 6
Shock Event Exclusion
Snow events, terminal shutdowns, system outages — flagged and excluded from training. Not predictable, not useful for patterns.

How Dimensions Layer Into a Prediction

PREDICTION FOR: Train I010, Bedford Park, Thursday March 5, 2026 // Step 1: IDENTIFY what kind of day this is day_of_week = Thursday ← DOMINANT signal week_position = 1st Thursday of March month_position = Early month (day 5 of 31) quarter = Q1 (historically lighter) year_position = Week 10 of 52 holiday_flag = NONE shock_flag = NONE // Step 2: GATHER comparable historical data points (all Thursdays) Last week Thursday (Feb 27) → 187 containers ← highest weight 2 weeks ago Thursday (Feb 20) → 192 containers 3 weeks ago Thursday (Feb 13) → 183 containers 4 weeks ago Thursday (Feb 6) → 179 containers Same-position Thursday last month → 175 containers Same-position Thursday 2 months → 168 containers Nearest Thursday last year (Mar 6, 2025) → 171 containers ← seasonality // Step 3: DETECT trend 4-week Thursday trend = +2.7 containers/week (↑ growing) // Step 4: WEIGHT and PREDICT Prior 1-2 weeks (same day): 40% weight → ~189 Prior 3-4 weeks (same day): 25% weight → ~181 Prior 2-3 months (avg): 20% weight → ~172 Year-over-year seasonal: 15% weight → ~171 PREDICTION: ~183 containers (confidence: ±8, range 175–191) TREND: ↑ Growing (recent weeks above historical average) // Step 5: COMPARE to allocation Current standing allocation: 150 | Current open: 20 | Total: 170 GAP: Predicted 183 vs Allocated 170 = +13 OVER-DEMAND RECOMMENDATION: Consider adding 15 open spaces Significance: 7.6% over allocation → ABOVE threshold → SURFACE TO INOC

Day-of-Week Anchoring — The Critical Design Principle

📍
Every temporal comparison snaps to the same weekday. "3rd Thursday of March" — not "March 20th." If March 20 is a Thursday one year and a Saturday the next, comparing raw dates produces garbage. The model always aligns on: same weekday → same position in month → same seasonal window.

Known Factors vs. Shock Events

Known Factors (Modeled)

✓Day of week (Monday through Sunday)
✓Position within month (1st/2nd/3rd/4th/5th occurrence of that weekday)
✓Month of year (seasonal curves)
✓Quarter (Q1-Q4 business cycles)
✓Holidays (fixed + floating, pre/during/post impact curves)
✓Peak season (Oct–Dec retail surge)
✓Trend direction (growing/declining/stable over recent weeks)

Shock Events (Excluded)

✗Snow/weather terminal shutdowns
✗Customer system outages
✗Equipment failures (terminal-wide)
✗Strike events / labor disruptions
✗Network disruptions (track closure)
✗Any operator-flagged anomaly day
🚩
Operators can tag any historical day as a shock event via a simple UI. Tagged days are excluded from all training windows so they don't pollute future predictions.

Prediction Engine — Models & Algorithms

Two-stage prediction: first predict gross demand per customer, then adjust for no-show behavior to arrive at net expected arrivals. All models are day-of-week anchored.

Stage 1: Demand Forecasting (Per Train, Per Day)

ModelTypeRoleWhy This Model
Weekday-Weighted HistoricalStatistical BaselineFoundation layer — same-weekday comparisons with recency weightingTransparent, explainable, validated against how operations already thinks
ProphetTime SeriesSeasonal decomposition + holiday effects + uncertainty intervalsHandles weekly/annual seasonality natively. Built-in holiday modeling. Produces confidence bands.
XGBoost / LightGBMGradient BoostingMulti-feature demand prediction using all temporal + customer + terminal featuresBest-in-class for tabular data. Fast inference. Feature importance via SHAP.
Ensemble (Stacking)Meta-ModelCombines all models — weighted by per-terminal accuracyReduces variance. Different models excel for different terminals/patterns.

Stage 2: No-Show Adjustment (Per Customer)

Day of weekNo-show rates vary by day — Friday/Saturday typically higherHigh
Reservation typeStanding vs. Open vs. Dynamic — different no-show profilesHigh
Waybill created?If waybill exists 1-4 days before departure, arrival is much more likelyHigh (leading indicator)
Days to departureReservations made far in advance have higher no-show ratesMedium

Trend shiftIs this customer's no-show rate improving or worsening?Medium
NET DEMAND FORMULA For each customer C on train T on day D: gross_demand(C, T, D) = predicted containers from Stage 1 noshow_prob(C, T, D) = predicted no-show probability from Stage 2 Net Expected Arrivals = Σ gross_demand(C,T,D) × (1 − noshow_prob(C,T,D)) Example: UPS standing: 50 allocated, predicted 48 gross, 12% no-show → 42.2 net JB Hunt standing: 30 allocated, predicted 22 gross, 35% no-show → 14.3 net Open customers: 40 allocated, predicted 35 gross, 8% no-show → 32.2 net Total Net Expected: ~89 containers vs. 120 allocated = 31 phantom-blocked slots

Anomaly Detection Layer

Isolation Forest

Detects unusual demand spikes or drops that don't fit any pattern. Flags for INOC review before recommendations are generated.

Changepoint Detection (CUSUM)

Detects when a customer's behavior fundamentally shifts — e.g., a shipper permanently reduces volume. Triggers model retrain for that customer segment.

Trend Analysis

Is demand on this corridor growing, declining, or stable? Rolling 4-week and 12-week trend indicators. Informs whether standing allocations need strategic review.

Model Retraining Cadence

1Continuous learning: Every actual outcome (INGAT/NOSHW) feeds back into the data lake automatically via Fabric mirroring
2Bi-weekly retrain: Models retrained every 2 weeks incorporating latest actuals + operator feedback (approve/reject patterns)
3Triggered retrain: If prediction accuracy drops below threshold (e.g., MAPE > 15% for 5 consecutive days), automatic retrain triggered
4Seasonal recalibration: Full model review at seasonal transitions (pre-peak Q4, post-holiday Q1)

Three Layers of Explainability

Layer 1: SHAP Feature Importance

For every single prediction, SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) calculates exactly how much each feature contributed. "Day-of-week contributed +18 containers. Customer UPS no-show trend contributed -6. Holiday proximity contributed -3." These are the mathematical facts behind the prediction.

Layer 2: Azure OpenAI Narrative Generation

Raw SHAP values are fed to Azure OpenAI (GPT-4) with a structured prompt that converts them into operator-friendly language: "Add 15 opens because last 4 Thursdays averaged 187 net arrivals, UPS utilization is trending at 90% on Thursdays, 3 waybills already created, and 4 standby requests suggest unmet demand." Every recommendation gets this narrative automatically.

Layer 3: Confidence Scoring

Each recommendation tagged High / Medium / Low confidence based on: prediction interval width, data availability for this train/day pattern, how many similar historical data points exist, and recent model accuracy for this terminal. Low-confidence flags encourage INOC scrutiny.

Example: What INOC Actually Sees

AI Explanation — Train I010 — Thursday March 5
✓ Recommendation: Add 15 Open Spaces
Predicted net arrivals: 183 (confidence range: 175–191) vs. current allocation of 170.

Why this prediction:
▸ Day-of-week pattern (44% of signal): Thursdays at Bedford Park consistently run 10-15% higher than the weekly average. Last 4 Thursdays: 192, 187, 183, 179 — averaging 185 net containers.
▸ Recent trend (22% of signal): Demand on this corridor is growing at ~2.7 containers/week over the past month. The trend is upward, not stable.
▸ Customer behavior (18% of signal): UPS Thursday utilization running at 90% (vs. 72% on Mondays). JB Hunt trending down slightly (-3% month-over-month). 3 waybills already created — strong arrival signal.
▸ Seasonal position (11% of signal): Early March historically shows a slight uptick as Q1 freight volumes recover post-holiday. Year-over-year same-position Thursday was 171 — we're running 7% above last year.
▸ Standby signal (5% of signal): 4 customers on standby for this train — indicates unmet demand beyond current allocation.

Confidence: HIGH — 48 comparable Thursdays in training data. Model accuracy on this train's Thursdays: 93.2% (7-day MAPE).

Risk if no action: ~13 containers turned away or pushed to standby. At incremental margin per container, this represents significant missed revenue. Space added now (7 hours before cutoff) has ~80% expected utilization rate.

Section 05

Functional Requirements

5.1 AI Demand Prediction

Per-train, per-day demand prediction anchored on all six temporal dimensions. Per-customer standing utilization forecasting. Net effective demand after no-show adjustment.

Prediction Outputs (Per Train, Per Day)

✓Net expected containers — the actual number operations should plan for
✓Gross reservations forecast — what reservation count will look like
✓Confidence interval — range (e.g., 175–191) so INOC knows certainty level
✓Trend direction — ↑ growing, → stable, ↓ declining
✓Gap analysis — predicted demand vs. current allocation = over/under

Per-Customer Breakdown

▸Each standing customer: predicted utilization for that day
▸No-show probability per customer for that specific weekday
▸Waybill status: has the customer created waybills yet? (leading indicator)
▸Customer trend: is this shipper shipping more or less recently?

5.2 Open Queue Automation

Automatically recommend adding or removing open spaces based on predicted demand vs. allocation — with configurable rules, limits, and human approval.

RULE 1: DEMAND EXCEEDS ALLOCATION IF predicted_net_demand > (standing_alloc + open_alloc) × 0.90 → RECOMMEND add opens Amount: predicted_gap + buffer (configurable, default 10%) RULE 2: SIGNIFICANT UNDERUTILIZATION IF predicted_net_demand < (standing_alloc + open_alloc) × 0.60 for 3+ days → FLAG for review Note: Do NOT auto-reduce. Surface for INOC strategic discussion. RULE 3: LEAD-TIME AWARE Space added ≥ 6 hours before cutoff → ~80% expected utilization Space added 2-4 hours before cutoff → ~40-60% expected utilization Space added < 2 hours before cutoff → ~15% expected utilization RECOMMENDATION: Prioritize early adds. Flag late adds as low-confidence. RULE 4: HOLIDAY ADJUSTMENT IF holiday_flag = TRUE → Apply holiday-specific demand curve Pre-Thanksgiving week: +15-25% on Tue/Wed, -40% on Thu/Fri Post-Christmas: -30% for 3-5 days RULE 5: DO NOT TOUCH IF terminal on "Do Not Touch" list → SUPPRESS all automation for that terminal/period // All rules configurable per terminal. All require INOC approval in Phase 1.

5.3 Standby / Waitlist Insights

Standby Queue Depth

How many standby requests exist per train? If automation adds opens, standby customers get served first (priority order). Track: how quickly are standby requests fulfilled?

Lead-Time-to-Utilization Analysis

When space is added X hours before cutoff, what percentage actually gets used? Find the sweet spot. This is a high-value deliverable INOC has explicitly asked for.

Unmet Demand Quantification

When trains show "full" but demand exists (standby queue + turned-away customers), quantify the revenue missed. This justifies the investment in better allocation.

5.4 Human-in-the-Loop Workflow

INOC operators review every AI recommendation. Approve, reject, or modify — with required explanation. Feedback improves the model.

✓Morning Briefing: At shift start, INOC sees today's predictions for all ~80 originating trains — only those exceeding significance threshold are highlighted
✓Three Actions: Approve (one-click) | Modify (adjust number, provide reason) | Reject (reason required)
✓Terminal Constraints: If terminal has flagged "Do Not Touch" (equipment down, etc.), recommendations for that terminal are suppressed
✓Feedback Loop: Every action stored. Rejection patterns analyzed weekly. Monthly retrain incorporates operator corrections.

5.5 Alerts & Shock Event Management

Automated Alerts

!Demand spike: prediction >2 std dev above rolling mean
!Customer behavior shift: no-show rate changes >15%
!Standby overflow: queue exceeds threshold
!Prediction accuracy degradation: MAPE >15% for 5+ days

Shock Event Tagging

✗INOC can tag any day as "shock event" — snow, outage, strike, etc.
✗Tagged days excluded from all future training windows
✗Categories: Weather, Equipment, Customer Outage, Network, Other
✗Can apply to single terminal, single train, or network-wide

5.6 Continuous Accuracy Tracking

Per Chang's requirement: clear definition of success, continuous accuracy measurement, visible improvement over time.

✓Predicted vs. Actual: Every prediction is scored against actual gate-in count next day. Automatic, continuous.
✓Accuracy by dimension: Break down accuracy by terminal, train, day-of-week, customer segment. Where is the model strong vs. weak?
✓Trend over time: Is accuracy improving week-over-week? Show the learning curve. Rising accuracy = model is getting better.
✓Feedback impact: Measure: do operator corrections improve subsequent predictions? Quantify the value of the human-in-the-loop.
